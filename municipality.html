Municipality of

Bacolor

Bacolor, situated in Pampanga, Philippines, is a town celebrated for its resilience despite frequent encounters with lahar flows from Mount Pinatubo. Its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and vibrant traditions, exemplified by the partially buried San Guillermo Parish Church, are a testament to its enduring spirit. Visitors can enjoy Kapampangan cuisine and partake in local festivals, like the Feast of San Guillermo, making Bacolor a cultural gem in the Philippines.

City Council

The Bacolor City Council is the governing body of this resilient Philippine municipality, working diligently to address the community's needs and uphold its rich heritage. Comprising dedicated members, the council plays a pivotal role in shaping Bacolor's future while preserving its cultural identity.

Mission

Through a well-crafted Development Agenda, committed public servants and responsive community, we shall all together achieve sustainable human and economic growth.

Vision

We envision Bacolor as the premier center of Kapampangan arts and culture, history and education, inhabited by God centered, resilient and empowered citizenry living in a safe and friendly environment, governed by a highly competitive and strong-willed leadership moving towards a dynamic center for sustainable human and socio-economic development.
