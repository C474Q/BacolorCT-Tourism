Pre Colonial

The pre-colonial history of Bacolor, Pampanga, is not well-documented, and the most of what is known is based on archaeological evidence and oral traditions. However, it is generally accepted that before Spanish invaders arrived, the region, like many other parts of the Philippines, was populated by diverse indigenous populations. The Philippines was home to a wide variety of communities with their own cultures, languages, and social structures before the Spanish colonization in the 16th century. In the lack of written records, archaeological discoveries, linguistic research, and oral traditions that have been passed down through the years have all contributed to our understanding of pre-colonial Bacolor. It is possible that Austronesian-speaking peoples who participated in agriculture, fishing, and trading once lived in the area that is now Bacolor. These groups had created sophisticated social and governmental structures. They maintained a variety of animism and indigenous religious practices while residing in stilted homes and trading with nearby settlements and islands. Bacolor, like many pre-colonial communities, would have been influenced by commerce and cultural exchanges with other adjacent areas, which would have led to the acquisition and adaptation of diverse cultural practices and technology.It's crucial to remember that pre-colonial historical accounts can change depending on regional mythology, oral traditions, and the viewpoints of various communities.
