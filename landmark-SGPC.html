San Guillermo Parish Church

San Guillermo Parish Church in Bacolor, Pampanga, Philippines, is dedicated to San Guillermo, the town's patron saint. Originally constructed in 1576 by the Augustinian Friars, the church's history is intertwined with the town's founding. Padre Diego de Ochoa, OSA, became its first parish priest two years later. After an earthquake in 1880, the church was demolished, but Fr. Eugenio Alvarez oversaw its reconstruction in 1886. Tragedy struck again in 1995 when a lahar flow from Mount Pinatubo, which had erupted in 1991, buried half of the church's 12-meter height. This disaster led to the evacuation of over 50,000 residents to safer areas. Adjacent to the church's façade, a museum offers insights into its history and features paintings depicting the 1991 Pinatubo eruption. The San Guillermo Parish Church stands as a testament to the enduring spirit of Bacolor's community, having weathered natural disasters and preserved its heritage throughout the centuries.
