Modern Day

Up until the last knowledge update in September 2021, the information provided remains current only until that date, lacking real-time awareness of developments in Bacolor, Pampanga beyond that point. Bacolor, Pampanga, has a significant history, notably marked by the 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo, causing extensive damage, including the burial of a substantial part of the town under volcanic ash and lahar, with the St. James the Apostle Parish Church serving as a poignant reminder of the disaster. Post-eruption, concerted efforts were made to rehabilitate Bacolor, addressing challenges in resettlement, economic recovery, and cultural heritage restoration, but the lasting effects persisted, necessitating ongoing endeavors. It is important to note that this information is based on the situation up until September 2021, and for the most up-to-date details about Bacolor, Pampanga's modern era, consulting local government sources, news outlets, or official town websites is recommended.
