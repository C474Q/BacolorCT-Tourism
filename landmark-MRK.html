Monument to Simón de Anda y Salazar

Simon de Anda y Salazar, born on October 28, 1701, served as the Spanish Governor General of the Philippines during the British invasion from October 1762 to March 1764. He established his governing center in Bacolor, Pampanga, which became the Philippines' capital for two years amid the British occupation. To honor his historical role in Bacolor, the Anda monument was erected and inaugurated on October 29, 2012. This monument commemorates Bacolor's 250th year anniversary as the Spanish Government's seat and was blessed in the presence of Hon. Jorge Domecq, the Ambassador of Spain to the Philippines.
